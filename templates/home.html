About Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that occurs when the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar (glucose). Glucose is the main source of energy for the body, and insulin is the hormone responsible for helping glucose enter the cells. If insulin production is low or the body cannot use insulin effectively, glucose builds up in the bloodstream, leading to high blood sugar levels.

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes occurs when the body either does not produce enough insulin or cannot use insulin properly. This causes increased blood sugar levels, which can damage organs over time.

Common Symptoms
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased thirst
  • Sudden weight loss
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow healing wounds
Prevention Tips
  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Control body weight
  • Avoid excessive sugar intake
  • Check blood sugar regularly
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol

Types of Diabetes

Diabetes is mainly classified into three major types. Each type has different causes and risk factors.

Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. People with Type 1 diabetes require insulin injections regularly.

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is the most common type. It happens when the body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin. It is mainly caused by obesity and unhealthy lifestyle.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes occurs during pregnancy. It usually disappears after delivery but increases the risk of developing Type 2 diabetes later in life.
